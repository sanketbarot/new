❓ FAQs

Fixed Deposit is a financial instrument provided by banks where you deposit money for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate, higher than regular savings.

FD uses compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt). Where A = maturity, P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency, t = years.
