❓ FAQs

Simple Interest (SI) is interest calculated only on the original principal. Unlike CI, it doesn't compound. Used in some loans and short-term deposits.

SI = (P × R × T) / 100
P = Principal, R = Rate per annum, T = Time in years
Total Amount = P + SI

SI is used in short-term loans, vehicle loans, personal loans, certain bonds, and educational loans. Most banks use CI for FD/RD.

SI: Linear growth (constant)
CI: Exponential growth (compounds)
For longer durations, CI is significantly higher!
