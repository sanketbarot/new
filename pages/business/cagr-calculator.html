❓ FAQs

Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) measures the mean annual growth rate over a period. Assumes growth compounds yearly.

CAGR = (Final/Initial)^(1/Years) - 1 × 100
Example: ₹100 to ₹200 in 5 years
CAGR = (200/100)^(1/5) - 1 = 14.87%

• Mutual Funds: 12-15% (long-term)
• Stocks: 10-15% (avg)
• Real Estate: 8-12%
• Business: 15-30%
• FD: 6-8%

CAGR accounts for compounding (real growth). Average returns is simple average (can be misleading). Always use CAGR for accurate comparison!
