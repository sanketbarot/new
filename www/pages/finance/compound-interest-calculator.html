❓ FAQs

Compound Interest (CI) is interest calculated on the initial principal AND accumulated interest. Often called "interest on interest" - powerful for wealth growth!

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
A = Maturity, P = Principal, r = Rate (decimal), n = Compounding frequency, t = Time in years

Compound Interest is always better for investments (you earn more). Simple Interest is better for loans (you pay less). Difference increases with time!
