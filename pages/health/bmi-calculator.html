❓ FAQs

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. Used to categorize underweight, normal, overweight, and obesity.

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height² (m²)
Example: 70 kg / (1.7m × 1.7m) = 24.2

• Under 18.5: Underweight
• 18.5 - 24.9: Normal weight
• 25 - 29.9: Overweight
• 30+: Obese

BMI is a general indicator. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, gender. Athletes may have high BMI but low body fat. Consult doctor for accurate assessment.
