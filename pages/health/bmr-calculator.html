❓ FAQs

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the calories your body burns at complete rest to maintain basic functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production.

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) = BMR × Activity Multiplier. It's the total calories you burn including all activities.

The Mifflin-St Jeor formula is most accurate (within 10%). Actual BMR varies based on muscle mass, genetics, and health conditions.

Weight Loss: Eat 500 cal less than TDEE (loses 0.5kg/week)
Weight Gain: Eat 500 cal more than TDEE
Maintenance: Eat exactly your TDEE
