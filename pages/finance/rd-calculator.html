❓ FAQs

Recurring Deposit is a savings scheme where you deposit a fixed amount monthly for a fixed period at predetermined interest, building lump sum at maturity.

RD uses formula: M = R × [(1+i)^n - 1] / (1 - (1+i)^(-1/3)). Compounded quarterly in most banks.

RD: For monthly savings habit. FD: For lump sum investment. FD usually gives slightly higher rates than RD.
