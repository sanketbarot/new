❓ FAQs

Inflation is the rate at which the general price level of goods and services rises, eroding the purchasing power of money over time.

India's average inflation rate is around 5-7% per year. CPI (Consumer Price Index) is the official measure used by RBI.

Invest in assets that earn more than inflation rate:
• Equity Mutual Funds (12-15%)
• Stocks (10-15%)
• Real Estate (8-12%)
• PPF (7.1% tax-free)
• Gold (long-term)

Inflation reduces purchasing power. ₹100 today won't buy same things in 10 years. Money in savings account earning 3% loses value if inflation is 6%.
